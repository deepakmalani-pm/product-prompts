### üìù Prompt: Conduct a Comprehensive Product Teardown

**Role:** You are a Product Manager at a [Insert your company type and scale] company based in [Insert geography or region]. You have deep expertise in the [Insert geography / region, sector, and consumer persona].

**Task:** Conduct a detailed teardown of the following [app / website / feature]. Your analysis should be critical, insightful, and focused on generating actionable ideas for our own product.

**Context:** The goal of this teardown is to deconstruct the user experience, understand the core value proposition, and identify strategic strengths and weaknesses. The final output should be a structured report.

**Product to Analyze:**
* **Name:** [Insert Product Name, e.g., "Srimandir App"]
* **Feature:** [Insert Specific Feature, e.g., "The pooja booking workflow"]
* **Link:** [Insert URL or App Store link]

**Format: Structure your teardown using these 6 sections:**

1.  **Core Value Proposition & Job-to-be-Done (JTBD):**
    * What is the primary "job" this product is hired to do for the user?
    * What is its stated value proposition? What is the *real* value proposition you observe?
    * Who is the clear target audience?
    * Define the ideal user persona

2.  **First-Time User Experience (FTUE) & Onboarding:**
    * Describe the user's "first 5 minutes."
    * How is the core value proposition communicated to a new user?
    * What are the key setup steps? How much friction is there?
    * What is the "Aha!" moment, and how quickly does the user get there?

3.  **Core User Flow Analysis:**
    * Describe the primary workflow for completing the product's main "job."
    * Where does the flow excel in terms of speed, clarity, or ease of use?
    * Where are the points of friction, confusion, or potential drop-off?

4.  **UI, UX, and Design Principles:**
    * What is your assessment of the visual design (UI)? Is it modern, dated, cluttered, clean?
    * How is the information architecture? Is it easy to navigate and find things?
    * What design patterns or "delight" factors stand out (e.g., micro-interactions, animations, copy)?

5.  **Strengths & Strategic Advantages (What to Learn):**
    * What 3-5 things does this product do exceptionally well?
    * What strategic advantages (e.g., ecosystem, data, brand) does this feature leverage?

6.  **Weaknesses & Opportunities (Actionable Insights):**
    * What 3-5 things does this product do poorly?
    * What user needs are clearly being unmet?
          
7. **Business numbers:**
    * what are daily and monthly active users? Split this data between overall, website, apps
    * What is daily and monthly orders and gross merchandise value (GMV)? Split this data between overall, own website, own apps, market places, quick commerce,          offline - general trade, offline - modern trade
    * What are the monthly retention numbers?
    * What is the gross margin of the business? Split this based on categories that business operate in
    * What is the customer acquisition costs and average order values of the business?
  
* Based on this analysis, what are key actionable opportunities or hypotheses we should consider for our own roadmap? What metrics and goals should be taken?
